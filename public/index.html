Research Paper Overview

"High-power transient beta events as early biomarkers of Alzheimer's disease conversion: An MEG study"

Key Finding

Reduced expression of beta-band (12-30 Hz) events—specifically lower rate, power, and duration in the precuneus—was predictive of future AD conversion and cognitive decline in MCI patients.

Study Cohort

85 MCI patients followed for 2.5 years, with 41 converting to Alzheimer's disease (CONV) and 44 remaining stable (NOCONV). Groups were matched for age, sex, and education.

Methodology

Resting-state MEG recordings analyzed for transient high-power oscillatory events in the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) and precuneus (PC), key hubs in the AD-related default mode network.

Significance

Event-based features offer a more precise interpretation of neural "slowing" than traditional averaged power metrics, potentially serving as early biomarkers for AD progression.
